Our inspirational slogan, "A house is a machine for living in," draws its origins from the visionary French Architect Le Corbusier. Le Corbusier was the pioneer of the concept of living in apartments or similar structures, a revolutionary idea during his time. Furthermore, his profound influence on the utilization of exposed concrete, commonly known as brutalism, has left an indelible mark on architectural history. Although not universally appreciated in modern times, some of his most iconic creations, such as Villa Savoye and the Chapel of Notre Dame du Haut, continue to captivate and inspire.

When we employ this slogan, our aim is to underscore the paramount significance of functional architecture. We firmly believe that one's home, where they spend their most cherished moments and invest their precious resources, should be meticulously designed to cater to their specific needs and desires.
